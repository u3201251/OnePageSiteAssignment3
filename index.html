Jobs within the Industry

GAME DESIGNER

- Conceptualising the rules, characters, worlds and stories for games.

- Use computer programming languages to write the code for the game.

- Protoyping new games.

- Pitching new game ideas to potential clients and investors.

- Education that is required is tyypically a Bachelor's degree in game design, computer science or computer engineering.

- Certifications in programming languages and a protfolio of sample projects is also helpful to stand out to possible employers.

GAME ARTIST

- Responsible for 2D and 3D art for video games.

- All visual elements involved in a game such as characters, objects, props, textures, assets, weapons, environments etc.

- Working from design briefs and concept art, the goal of game artists is to bring the world to life and populate the world of the game from the storyboard.

- Specialization of roles like character design, architecture and landscapes, special effects, animation, lighting etc.

- A degree isn't required as employers will judge applications based on their epxerience and portfolio but relevant degrees like game art and computer games modelling do help.

- A few of the programs out there to become familiar with include Maya, Blender, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe After Effects and CAD to name a few.

GAME ENGINEER

- Through the use of computer software and hardware, Game Engineers write and modify programming code to create game framework and individual elements.

- Testing specific elements or products and implementing improvements.

- Working within a team of engineers, designers and programmers to tackle problems and come up with solutions.

- Making sure games are properly designed and can work on various pieces of harward like PCs, Consoles or Mobile devices.

- A Bachelor's degree in computer science or software development.

- Internship programs through schools at game studios help develop your skills earlier on to give you an advantage over other candidates.

GAME MARKETING

- Managing the promotion of the video game through marketing actions.

- Developing a marketing startergy to target audience by reasearching online communities and the type of content that appeals to them.

- Reasearching about potential competitive companies and building hype around your video games release to increase purchases/downloads.

- Roles under this position include Social Media marketing, Email marketing, Mobile marketing, Content marketing and connecting with high level influencers in the relevant areas to further promote the product.

- Game marketing typically requires individuals to have a bachleor's degree in either marketing, public relations, media relations or advertising.

- Skills that are required for the role include data analysis, communication, organisation, strategic thinking and collaboration in groups.
